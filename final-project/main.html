An Unspoken Love Story

At the age of 15, a boy named Jet changed my life. He showed me my purpose: to advocate for those without a voice. Jet has Down Syndrome and he struggled to verbalize his love for others, but I never felt we needed words to communicate.

In the countless hours we spent together, Jet had never said my name; I wasn't sure he even knew who I was. But every game of catch we played, every book we read together, was a connection being built.

One day, he broke down his barriers and said, "Awiyah?" meaning Riya. Since that day, Jet remembers the name of every person he interacts with.

Why UXR?

Jet is the reason I am who I am today and why I want to be a UX Researcher working within the assistive technology space.

Illuminating Abilities since 2008

Angels on Stage is a nonprofit organization where people with special needs show off their talents through musical theater. Whether that's teaching them a dance or helping them press a button on a tablet that speaks their line, we are there solely to illuminate their abilities.

My Journey to HCI

Being part of these beautiful “Angel moments” illuminated a path towards applied behavioral analysis therapy, or so I thought. It wasn’t until halfway through my undergraduate studies, that I discovered the world of technology. I learned about the power of visual storytelling through analytics and how data can reveal insights that would normally elude the naked eye. I began building my skills in coding, statistics, and even advanced modeling.

My Love for Research

As an Undergraduate Research Assistant in Dr. LouAnne Boyd’s lab, I explored data science in the wild, investigating how to alter visual perception in individuals with Autism. I stepped outside my comfort zone, learning to architect databases, perform image processing in Python, and model data in R. This effort culminated in a peer-reviewed journal article. By senior year, I had mastered these skills, so Dr. Boyd and I launched an empirical research study to advance our published work. Over a year, we designed the study, ran participants, and analyzed findings, while ensuring ethical practices and accommodating each participant's unique accessibility needs.

The Numbers

In my industry work, I centered technology design around users. As a Data Analyst on an agile team, I used informal UX research methods—interviews, task analysis, and prototype testing—to enhance dashboard analytics. By redesigning the information architecture and coding the back end, I streamlined an over-engineered dashboard into an intuitive data story, boosting adoption by 50%. Conversations and shadowing with sales managers, salespeople, and business leaders informed a redesign that aligned the tool with their priorities while delivering actionable insights.
